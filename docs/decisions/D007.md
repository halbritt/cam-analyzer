# D007 — Conformance by adversary corpus

Status: proposed (★ round-1 non-obvious-but-viable pick)
Date: 2026-06-16
Source: [`design/ROUND1_SYNTHESIS.md`](../design/ROUND1_SYNTHESIS.md) §3, cluster D

## Decision

The durable asset of cam-analyzer is **not** the `CamProfile` interface — it is a
**frozen suite of traps a profile must refuse**. C1 (one-way dependency), C3
(measured ≠ inferred), and C4 (hot-swap) are enforced by:

1. a `cam_analyzer.conformance` corpus — a museum of physically- or
   semantically-invalid profiles/cards that a conformant implementation must
   *reject or be unable to construct*; and
2. an **import guard** — a test asserting no module under `analysis/` imports
   anything from `sources/`.

Correctness is *defined by the attacks the boundary withstands*, not by the happy
path. The spec grows by adversary, not only by feature.

## Why this and not "more tests" or "a validator"

The round-1 adversarial-boundary branch showed that the obvious answers — add a
cam-card consistency validator, write more unit tests, add a confidence field and
ask implementers to fill it honestly — are exactly what the adversary walks
through. They rely on reviewer vigilance. The corpus instead makes "well-typed ⟹
physically-coherent, honestly-labeled" a *tested property*: a profile that can be
constructed while feeding analysis inconsistent, mislabeled, fabricated, or
sparse-pretending-continuous numbers is, by definition, non-conformant.

It is the only one of the four round-1 clusters that keeps the other three
(typed boundary, canonical representation, per-region fitness) honest as the
codebase grows — which is why the synthesis starred it.

## Consequences

- A `conformance/` package and `tests/test_architecture_boundary.py` are
  first-class, shipped artifacts — part of the spec, not an afterthought.
- New sources and analyses are "done" only when they pass the corpus.
- Trap names are part of the [ubiquitous language](../reference/ubiquitous-language.md):
  `mm_labeled_as_inch`, `never_closes`, `sparse_as_continuous`,
  `seam_phantom_jerk`, `analysis_imports_source`, …

## Seed traps (initial corpus)

- non-monotone-then-returns lift; never-closes lift;
- `mm`-labeled-as-`inch`; `advertised_duration < duration@0.050″`;
- sparse-lookup masquerading as continuous;
- `CompositeProfile` seam injecting phantom jerk (cross-refs [D010](decision-log.md));
- an `analysis/` module importing from `sources/` (the C1 leak).

## Open

The corpus does not by itself solve D012 (ergonomics-as-integrity) or D013
(honesty under discontinuity); it is the harness that will *test* whatever round 2
decides for those.
